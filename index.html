Fathoms

Emotionally charged words known as Fathoms. Fathoms are a starting point to inspire creativity; from a place of structure comes absolute freedom. The Editorial team will create seasonal Fathoms.

Users can navigate through stories filtered by individual Fathoms to discover the inventions of others.



Some Fathoms include:<Tail>, <Zombie>, <Alien>, <Robot>, <Demon>, <Kingdom>, <Lipstick>, <Rainbow>, <Basement>, <River>, <Scissors>, <Crystal-ball>, <Cherub>, <Mirror>, <Wheatfield>...



What We Do

A literary platform for writers to create micro-fiction inspired by unique Fathoms that tap into the psyche. Each word choice must be deliberate and potent.


With Fathoms in hand you create narratives: 55 or 415 word stories.







Meet The Editorial Team

SARAH ELIZABETH SCHANTZ

Editor-in-Chief
& Co-Founder
Email
Sarah Elizabeth Schantz literally grew up in a bookstore in Boulder, Colorado. Her novel, Fig, debuted from Simon & Schuster and was selected by NPR as a Best Read of 2015 before winning the 2016 Colorado Book Award. She is a two-time Pushcart Prize nominee and her award-winning short fiction and essays have been widely published by literary journals such as, but not limited to The Los Angeles Review, Third Coast, and Midwestern Gothic. She currently lives in an old farmhouse on the outskirts of Boulder where she is surrounded by open sky, century-old cottonwoods, and coyote. She teaches literature and creative writing at Front Range Community College and runs her own private workshop series, (W)rites of Passage. For more information about Schantz herself please visit: www.SarahElizabethSchantz.org and for more information regarding her seasonally-inspired workshops please visit: www.WritesofPassage.org.

MAIREAD CASE

Associate Editor
Email
Mairead Case is a working writer in Colorado. She is a PhD student at the University of Denver, the Summer Writing Program Coordinator at Naropa, a legal observer, and a writing teacher at DU, Naropa, and the Denver women's jail. Before Colorado Mairead lived in Chicago for a decade, where she worked at the Poetry Foundation, the Neighborhood Writing Alliance, and the Public Media Institute, among other places. Mairead is the author of the novel See You In the Morning (featherproof) and the poetry chapbook Tenderness (Meekling Press). Her website is maireadcase.tumblr.com.

TONI OSWALD

Assistant Editor
Email
Toni Oswald is a writer, musician, performer, filmmaker, and visual artist,who currently lives in Boulder, Colorado with her partner Max Davies and their cat Kiki Pamplemousse Fontaine. Her work has been shown in Los Angeles, Amsterdam, and New York. She has performed across the United States and Europe with the punk rock performance-art theater group Theater Carnivale, as well as with musicians and performers such as Anne Waldman, John Frusciante, Max Davies, Ambrose Bye, Johanna Went, Thurston Moore, Josh Klinghoffer, Cecilia Vicuña, River Phoenix, Junior Burke, Stephen Holman, and Spike Jones, among others. Her work has been published in magazines here and abroad, and her books include the graphic novel A Loveletter to the Transformer and a poetry collection Things Are Much Stranger Than We Had Imagined. She is currently working on a novel about a fourteen-year-old girl clown set in the 1950's, entitled Lonesome in A False World. You can also find her musical altar ego, The Diary of Ic Explura, on Bandcamp.

JADE LASCELLES

Jade Lascelles is a poet, editor, and letterpress printer currently based in Boulder, Colorado. She serves as Editor-in-Chief of the Naropa University literary journal Bombay Gin, and is a founding member and editor at the Precipice publishing collective. In addition to a career in editing, she teaches courses on writing and small press publishing and manages the Harry Smith Print Shop at Naropa.
