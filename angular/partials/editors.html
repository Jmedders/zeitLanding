SARAH ELIZABETH SCHANTZ

Co-Founder
& Editor-in-Chief
Contact for a call for submission
Sarah Elizabeth Schantz literally grew up in a bookstore in Boulder, Colorado. Her novel, Fig, debuted from Simon & Schuster and was selected by NPR as a Best Read of 2015 before winning the 2016 Colorado Book Award. She is a two-time Pushcart Prize nominee and her award-winning short fiction and essays have been widely published by literary journals such as, but not limited to The Los Angeles Review, Third Coast, and Midwestern Gothic. She currently lives in an old farmhouse on the outskirts of Boulder where she is surrounded by open sky, century-old cottonwoods, and coyote. She teaches literature and creative writing at Front Range Community College and runs her own private workshop series, (W)rites of Passage. For more information about Schantz herself please visit: www.SarahElizabethSchantz.org and for more information regarding her seasonally-inspired workshops please visit: www.WritesofPassage.org.

TONI OSWALD

Creative Consultant
& Assistant Editor
Contact for a call for submission
Toni Oswald is a writer, musician, performer, filmmaker, and visual artist,who currently lives in Boulder, Colorado with her partner Max Davies and their cat Kiki Pamplemousse Fontaine. Her work has been shown in Los Angeles, Amsterdam, and New York. She has performed across the United States and Europe with the punk rock performance-art theater group Theater Carnivale, as well as with musicians and performers such as Anne Waldman, John Frusciante, Max Davies, Ambrose Bye, Johanna Went, Thurston Moore, Josh Klinghoffer, Cecilia Vicuña, River Phoenix, Junior Burke, Stephen Holman, and Spike Jonze, among others. Her work has been published in magazines here and abroad, and her books include the graphic novel A Loveletter to the Transformer and a poetry collection Things Are Much Stranger Than We Had Imagined. She is currently working on a novel about a fourteen-year-old girl clown set in the 1950's, entitled Lonesome in A False World. You can also find her musical altar ego, The Diary of Ic Explura, on Bandcamp.

JADE LASCELLES

Assistant Editor
Contact for a call for submission
Jade Lascelles is a poet, editor, and letterpress printer currently based in Boulder, Colorado. She serves as Editor-in-Chief of the Naropa University literary journal Bombay Gin, and is a founding member and editor at the Precipice publishing collective. In addition to a career in editing, she teaches courses on writing and small press publishing and manages the Harry Smith Print Shop at Naropa. She holds an MFA in Writing and Poetics, and her work has been featured in publications such as Gesture and Periodical. Her manuscript, Proximate Seams, and a co-edited anthology from Precipice are both forthcoming.

ERIN JENDRAS

Erin Jendras writes speculative fiction, horror, and magical realism. She has been published in Cicada magazine, the Not Your Average Monster Anthology, several online journals, and in 2007 she received a fellowship award to the Vermont Studio Center for her fiction and poetry. When Erin isn’t writing or reading, you can find her drinking copious amounts of coffee, enjoying the Rocky Mountain Wilderness, and mothering a tiny human who is more fantastic and miraculous than any of the creatures she writes about. For more information about Erin, please visit www.linkedin.com/in/erinjendras.
